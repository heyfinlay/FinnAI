# Workflow Before / After Examples

## 1. Mortgage broker enquiry handling
### Before
- Enquiries arrive through web forms, email, and referral partners with no single triage rule
- First response timing depends on who notices the lead first
- Brokers or admins repeatedly ask for the same missing information
- Clients chase updates because milestone communication is inconsistent

### After
- New enquiries are acknowledged and assigned inside a defined response window
- Qualification prompts and next actions follow a standard sequence
- Document requests and reminders are structured by stage
- Stage changes trigger cleaner client and internal updates

### Outcome
Less lead leakage, fewer avoidable interruptions, and better broker time allocation.

---

## 2. Accounting client onboarding
### Before
- Client details are gathered across email threads, PDFs, and ad hoc calls
- Staff copy the same context into job notes, spreadsheets, and task systems
- Missing-information follow-ups rely on manual reminders
- Internal ownership is blurry once work is underway

### After
- Intake details land in a structured onboarding flow
- Standard client comms reduce manual rewriting
- Missing-information prompts happen consistently
- Ownership is clearer at each stage of onboarding and recurring work

### Outcome
Lower admin drag, more consistent client experience, and less senior attention wasted on coordination.
