# FinnAI Outreach System — Audit Offer

## Core thesis
Outbound should sell the **problem diagnosis**, not the implementation. The message is stronger when it points to a visible operational pain: slow follow-up, repetitive admin, reporting drag, or messy handoffs.

## Priority niches
1. Mortgage brokers
2. Accountants / bookkeepers
3. Professional service firms with intake, follow-up, and admin-heavy delivery

## Trigger signals to personalise against
- Strong reviews + obvious growth signals
- Online lead capture forms but weak speed-to-lead evidence
- Multi-step service delivery with admin/compliance overhead
- Team expansion or multiple offices
- Founder/partner visible as bottleneck

## Email sequence
### Email 1 — Pattern interrupt
**Subject:** quick thought on {{business_name}}’s workflow

Hi {{first_name}},

Had a look at {{business_name}}. Firms like yours usually don’t have an AI problem — they have a workflow problem that AI either fixes or makes worse.

The pattern I keep seeing is admin-heavy teams losing time in 3 places:
- lead follow-up and enquiry handling
- internal handoffs / chasing info
- repetitive client comms and reporting

I run a one-day AI Efficiency Audit to map where that drag is coming from and where AI is actually worth using.

If useful, I can send over a 3-bullet view of where I’d inspect first for {{business_name}}.

— Finlay

### Email 2 — Specificity follow-up
**Subject:** where I’d look first at {{business_name}}

Hi {{first_name}},

A few workflow areas I’d normally inspect first for a business like yours:
1. response speed from new enquiry to next action
2. repeated admin across onboarding / fulfilment / follow-up
3. internal handoffs where context gets lost or delayed

That usually surfaces a few immediate wins and a much smaller number of AI opportunities actually worth implementing.

If you want, I can walk you through the audit structure on a quick call.

— Finlay

### Email 3 — Close / soft break-up
**Subject:** should I close the loop?

Hi {{first_name}},

I’ll leave it here after this.

If reducing admin drag, missed follow-up, or workflow mess is on the radar this quarter, the audit is probably the simplest starting point before building anything.

Happy to send a short summary or a booking link if timing is right.

— Finlay

## Loom / DM angle
`Noticed you’re in an admin-heavy service business. That usually means there are 2–3 workflow bottlenecks where AI helps a lot — and another 5 places where it just adds noise. I run a one-day audit to separate those.`

## CTA options
- Want a 3-bullet teardown?
- Want the audit outline?
- Worth a 20-minute fit call?
- Should I send the booking link?

## Outreach rules
- Keep the message diagnosis-led, not tech-led.
- Don’t lead with “automation”. Lead with missed speed, admin drag, and messy handoffs.
- Use niche relevance sparingly but clearly.
- Personalise one line, not the whole email.
